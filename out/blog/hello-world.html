Ooops, I did it again. This is my 6th personal website revamp in the last 4 years because I just can't seem to make a commitment and stick to it long term üôà

But Why?

My last site was a simple Jekyll blog where I wanted to publicly document my journey in the product/tech industry as it unfolded. And while I largely succeeded in that aspect by maintaining extremely detailed logs in my Notion workspace, I haven't done them justice by keeping them to my eyes only.

My hypothesis: the friction between me typing out stuff in Notion and sharing it online was too much. I didn't want to have to deal with creating a properly formatted markdown file ‚Üí adding metadata/front matter to it ‚Üí pushing it to github branch every time I wanted to publish something.

New Plan

Since I was already writing stuff in Notion and it practically ran on the web, I took a page out of Aakash Goel's book and decided to try automating as much of my publishing process as I could, and take away all my excuses once and for all.

And how better to learn the basics of React, than to simply build a personal website using it? So that's exactly what I set out to make.

A scalable website that is light on design but painless to maintain. One that will grow right alongside me as I navigate my career.

What you're currently looking at, is my progress so far. A super simple blog made with Gatsby, that's almost entirely managed from within Notion. And while it's far from complete, here's some of my wins that I'm very proud of so far:

  1. I now have a solid grasp of the basics of React
  1. I'm no longer afraid of data handling as much as I was just a few months ago
  1. My understanding of typography scales is vastly improved
  1. I implemented a fully 'fluid' responsive design with ZERO media queries
  1. I live-streamed a majority of the process on Youtube, it built my confidence and also held me publicly accountable
  1. ...and a lot more that I'll document as I go

At the end, whether this adventure will prove or disprove my original hypothesis still remains to be seen of course. But I'm feeling positive this time around. üë®‚Äçüîß
